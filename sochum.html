Topic Area Summary

Topic 1 Capital Punishment with Special Emphasis on Juvenile Delinquency

Capital Punishment can be loosely defined as - the lawful infliction of death as a punishment; the death penalty. For years, the world has had heated debate on the morality and ethical neutrality of capital punishment, and its political and social repercussions. The discussion as to whether it is humane to execute a person and its effectiveness in deterring crime is still being questioned. With the evolution of thought and technology, and country’s opting for other forms of punishment; the United Nations has also revised and updated its stance. Although human rights laws guarantee to every person the ‘Right to Life’ it does not prohibit the use of the death penalty as a punishment for crimes but does encourage its abolition and seeks to limit its use.

Recently, our country has seen a wave of protests against the system, encouraging the death penalty to be meted out not just in ‘the rarest of rare’ cases.

Delegates are encouraged to look deep into the socio-political and religious situations of their country, alternatives to capital punishment and terrorist activity and especially look into the question of meting out the death penalty to juveniles.

Topic 2 Role of Media in War Zones

Since Hitler’s exploitation of the media in the Second World War and various instances of governments using the media for war propaganda and the recent Arab Spring which was triggered off by the social media, the media’s effectiveness in influencing large sections of society (including domestic and international politics) cannot be denied. In this age of technology and a free thinking society, the importance of the media and its social responsibility has come into question. The role of the media in war zones and areas of armed conflict are especially crucial as divulging of information can be both a help as well as a hindrance. As a committee, we will look into the rules and regulations that should be imposed on the media (television, newspaper, internet, etc.), their limitations and how to effectively use the media in times of need.
